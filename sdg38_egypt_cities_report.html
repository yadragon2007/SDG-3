What is SDG Target 3.8?

SDG Target 3.8 calls for universal health coverage (UHC) — including financial risk protection, access to quality essential health-care services, and access to safe, effective, quality and affordable essential medicines and vaccines for all. It is measured by the UHC Service Coverage Index (0–100) and the share of population with catastrophic health expenditure.

Key finding: Egypt's national UHC index of 67 is below the global average of 71 and significantly below the First World average of 85. The most critical barrier is financial protection — out-of-pocket spending (~60% of all health expenditure) exposes Egyptian families to catastrophic health costs, particularly in non-urban areas where the Universal Health Insurance Law (2018) phase-in has not yet reached. The UHC reform started in Port Said in 2018 and is being rolled out gradually across all 27 governorates.

⚠ Financial catastrophe gap: An estimated 3.2 million Egyptians face catastrophic health expenditure annually — defined as spending >10% of household consumption on health. Rural households in Upper Egypt (especially Sohag, Assiut, Qena, and Luxor governorates) and the border governorates carry the highest burden. Out-of-pocket payments of ~60% are triple the WHO-recommended ceiling of ≤20%.

Pattern of inequality: A stark geographic divide is visible — all 4 Critical governorates are in Upper Egypt, while the 6 Fair governorates are all in the Greater Cairo/Nile Delta/Canal Zone. Upper Egypt's governorates score 20–35 points below Cairo on the UHC sub-index, translating to dramatically different health outcomes. This mirrors the urban–rural access disparities described across project documents, including studies on secondary/tertiary healthcare access challenges and CAPMAS health data.

Policy Recommendations
Priority Actions to Advance SDG 3.8 in Egypt

Based on this analysis and WHO EMRO guidance, the following evidence-based interventions are recommended, ranked by urgency and feasibility for Egypt's context.

Immediate (0–2 years)
Critical Zone Intervention
  • Accelerate UHI Law rollout in Sohag, Qena, Assiut, Luxor
  • Deploy 1,000+ physicians to Upper Egypt via incentive programs
  • Establish emergency referral corridors to tertiary hospitals
  • Expand 100-Million Seha program NCD screening in rural Upper Egypt
  • Establish community health worker (CHW) networks
Short-term (2–4 years)
Financial Protection
  • Reduce OOP spending from 60% to below 40% by 2027
  • Complete UHI Phase 2 rollout across all Delta governorates
  • Expand generic medicine availability in public facilities
  • Launch targeted subsidies for rural households below poverty line
  • Regulate private sector pricing for essential services
Medium-term (4–7 years)
System Strengthening
  • Increase health expenditure from 4.5% to 6%+ of GDP
  • Double physician density toward WHO minimum of 23/10k
  • Establish telemedicine and digital health networks in remote gov.
  • Build specialist training centers in Upper Egypt
  • Complete full national UHI coverage by 2028
