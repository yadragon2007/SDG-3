Section 01 — Introduction

What is SDG Target 3.1?

Sustainable Development Goal 3 — Good Health and Well-Being — contains 13 targets. Target 3.1 specifically aims to reduce the global Maternal Mortality Ratio (MMR) to fewer than 70 maternal deaths per 100,000 live births by 2030, and to have all countries achieve an MMR of at least 140 or less. It also calls for at least 90% of births to be attended by skilled health personnel.

Global Summary: In 2020, an estimated 287,000 women died from pregnancy or childbirth-related causes — nearly 800 per day. This represents a 34% reduction from 2000, but far short of the pace needed to hit the 2030 SDG target. The COVID-19 pandemic disrupted maternal health services worldwide, reversing years of progress in many nations, including Egypt.

"Assiut and Sohag are at MMR rates of 62 and 50 respectively — higher than Egypt's national average of 43 — and still have significant ground to cover to achieve their 2030 targets."
— Egypt SDG Progress Report, MATEC Conferences, 2023

Section 02 — Global & Regional Context

How Egypt Compares:
World, Middle East & Africa

Egypt sits at a critical crossroads — well above first-world MMR figures but below the Sub-Saharan African average. Progress since 1992 has been remarkable (from 174 to ~24 per 100,000 by 2022), but COVID-19 caused a sharp spike in 2021, and persistent regional disparities within Egypt remain a serious challenge.

MMR by Egyptian Governorate: 2020 vs. 2021 vs. 2026 Target

SOURCE: Scientific Reports 2025, CAPMAS, UNFPA Egypt | Per 100,000 live births

Key Finding: Upper Egypt and border governorates bear a disproportionate share of maternal deaths. Assiut recorded 37.7% of all studied maternal deaths during 2020–2021. COVID-19 caused the MMR in Fayoum to surge from 39.25 to 71.65 and in Kafr El-Sheikh from 36.44 to 56.6. Rural residence in Lower Egypt was found to multiply COVID-associated maternal mortality risk by 5× (aOR = 5.15). Hospital referral refusals were the single strongest risk factor (aOR = 8.72), pointing to systemic failures in the referral chain.

Section 04 — First-World Solutions for Egypt

Transferable Models from High-Income Countries

Northern and Western European countries achieve MMRs of 1–5 per 100,000 — more than 10× better than Egypt's national average, and 15–40× better than Egypt's worst-performing governorates. Their models offer concrete, adaptable lessons for Egypt's healthcare reform agenda, particularly through the "Haya Karima" initiative and Egypt Vision 2030.

Norway integrates midwives as the primary caregivers for low-risk pregnancies. Community-based maternity clinics are distributed across remote regions, ensuring geographic equity. Parental leave (49 weeks at full pay) reduces postnatal stress.

Egypt Application Establish community midwifery centers in Upper Egypt governorates (Assiut, Sohag, Qena). Train 5,000+ midwives under MOHP's Family Health Strategy. Embed in "Haya Karima" village units in the poorest 5,000 villages.

Dutch midwives outnumber ob-gyns and provide the majority of prenatal care and deliveries. The Netherlands' strong primary care network acts as the backbone of maternal health, with clear referral pathways to secondary care when needed.

Egypt Application Restructure Egypt's ob-gyn-heavy model to integrate midwifery cadres at PHC level. Redesign referral chain protocols to eliminate the 8.72× risk multiplier of referral refusals, a leading cause of Egypt's COVID-era maternal deaths.

Finland's "baby box" program ensures every expectant mother, regardless of income, receives a starter kit of newborn essentials. The universal public health ANC system mandates minimum antenatal visit standards enforced nationally.

Egypt Application Scale Egypt's existing Maternal and Child Health (MCH) program to guarantee minimum 8 ANC visits (currently only 90% of women attend at all; deep rural rates are <70%). Launch an Egyptian "Maternal Box" program targeting Sohag and North Sinai.

The UK's MBRRACE-UK program conducts confidential enquiries into every maternal death, producing actionable national reports. This evidence-based feedback loop has driven systemic improvements in obstetric care quality over decades.

Egypt Application Strengthen Egypt's existing Maternal Death Surveillance & Response (MDSR) system — currently active under MOHP — to produce governorate-level annual reports. Mandate hospital review committees for all maternal deaths in Assiut, Sohag, Beni-Suef, Qena.

Japan's Maternal & Child Health Handbook (since 1948) has evolved into a digital health record. Every pregnant woman carries a standardized passport tracking ANC visits, labs, and risk factors, enabling continuity of care across providers.

Egypt Application Digitize Egypt's existing Mother and Child Health Cards across all PHC units. Integrate with the national Universal Health Coverage (UHC) IT system. Deploy telemedicine in border governorates (North Sinai, Matrouh) where specialist access is critically limited.

Sri Lanka dramatically reduced MMR over 60 years through long-term government commitment to expanding a network of trained midwives in every village, free obstetric care, and transport subsidies. A comparable-income country to Egypt demonstrating that systemic investment works.

Egypt Application Deploy a "Midwife Per Village" program in Egypt's poorest 1,000 villages under Haya Karima. Provide subsidized transport vouchers for pregnant women in Upper Egypt to reach facilities. Replicate Sri Lanka's sustained 60-year political commitment framework via Egypt Vision 2030.
